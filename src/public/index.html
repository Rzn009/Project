About Quantum

Quantum is a domain in physics that studies the behavior of very small particles, such as atoms and subatomic particles. This theory describes the properties of these particles in terms of probability, where a particle has no definite state until it is measured or observed. Unique phenomena such as superposition and entanglement are characteristic of quantum physics.

The physicist

Niels Bohr

German-American physicist who formulated the special and general theories of relativity, and is known for his contributions to quantum theory and the photoelectric effect.
Albert Einstein

Danish physicist who contributed to Bohr's model of the atom and received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1922.
Isaac Newton

English scientist best known for his laws of motion, law of universal gravitation, and contributions to calculus.
